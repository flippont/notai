• 1. Future Simple (Futur Simple):
    Used to express actions or events that will happen in the future.
    Formed by adding specific endings to the infinitive verb.
    Regular verbs follow the pattern: infinitive + endings (-ai, -as, -a, -ons, -ez, -ont).
    Example: Je parlerai (I will speak).
  • 2. Near Future (Futur Proche):
    Used to express actions or events that are going to happen soon.
    Formed by using the present tense of the verb "aller" (to go) followed by the infinitive of the main verb.
    Example: Je vais parler (I am going to speak).
  • 3. Passé Composé with "Avoir" (Compound Past with "To Have"):
    Used to express completed actions or events in the past.
    Formed by conjugating the auxiliary verb "avoir" in the present tense, followed by the past participle of the main verb.
    Example: J'ai parlé (I spoke/I have spoken).
  • 4. Passé Composé with "Être" (Compound Past with "To Be"):
    Used for specific verbs of motion, reflexive verbs, and a few other verbs.
    Formed by conjugating the auxiliary verb "être" in the present tense, followed by the past participle of the main verb.
    Example: Je suis allé(e) (I went/I have gone).
  • 5. Irregular Past Participles:
    Some verbs have irregular past participles in the passé composé.
    Examples: boire (bu), prendre (pris), voir (vu), etc.
  • 6. Imperative:
    Used to give commands or make requests.
    The imperative form usually corresponds to the second-person singular (tu), first-person plural (nous), and second-person plural (vous) forms of the present tense.
    Example: Parle (Speak), Parlons (Let's speak), Parlez (Speak/You all speak).
  • 7. Present Tense:
    Used to express actions or states happening in the present.
    Regular verbs follow specific endings for each subject pronoun.
    Example: Je parle (I speak/I am speaking).
