Regular Verbs

Is a past tense to describe once off completed actions in the past. E.g, I ate my lunch at 1 p.m. 

This is a composite tense. It is made of more than 1 verb. 

To put regular verbs in the passé, compose, we use: 

Avoir in the present tense + Past Participle 

The past participle 

The P.P is easy to use as it does not change depending on the pronoun. 

E.g. I have eaten, she has eaten. 

Eaten is the past participle.

Negation  

TO make a passe compose sentence negative, we use the order: 

Pronoun + NE + avoir + PAS + Past participle 

E.g. J’ai parlé avec mon grand-père => Je n’ai pas parlé avec mon grand-pére
