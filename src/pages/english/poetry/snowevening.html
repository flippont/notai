Summary of the poem:

The narrator of this poem is walking through the woods on a snowy evening. He stops there for a while and thinks of how lovely it is. He would like to stay there, but he has things to do, and must travel on.

Deeper Meaning?

It’s very well to spend time doing nothing, admiring the world around you, but eventually you must get on with life, and fulfil your commitments.

What’s the point of this poem?

The beauty of nature, and the ‘real world’ where you have to do the things you’ve promised.

What is Frost’s obsession with nature?

Frost found that nature could mean many different things. Just as in ‘The Road Not Taken’, he used nature to represent two different choices in life, he uses nature here to describe the desire to hide away from commitments and responsibilities.

What’s the main technique?

Euphony: the flow of poem as well as the sweet-sounding words capture the peace and beauty of the snow-covered woods: “The only other sound’s the shake/ Of easy wind and downy snow.”

Important image?

The image of the woods, covered in snow. Frost captures its beauty, and we, like the reader, see the appeal of staying in such a place. He is alone, with only his horse for company, and there is no-one to trouble him:
“He will not see me stopping here/ To watch his woods fill up with snow.”

Important line?

“The woods are lovely, dark and deep /But I have promises to keep/ And miles to go before I sleep/ And miles to go before I sleep/”
This captures the desire to stay in the woods, away from people and problems and responsibilities: but he has to go on; has things to do before he can rest.
