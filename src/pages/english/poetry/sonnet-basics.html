Modern-English Translation

Will I compare you to a summer’s day? You are lovelier and steadier Sometimes storms will ruin a summer’s day Sometimes it’s too hot And the next minute the sun is gone Summer will eventually change into something else Things change, whether it’s nature or chance But you will stay perfect You’ll never lose your beauty When you die, death will find no joy in in/Your beauty is deathless (Two possible meanings here) You will live forever in poetry As long as this poem is read You too will live.

Bite-sized Summary

Summer is nice, but it can change, and it has to end eventually. The muse of this poem is better than a summer’s day, as his beauty will never die. As long as this poem is read, his beauty will live forever.

What’s the point of this poem?

Celebrating a beauty that will last forever. What’s the main technique? Extended metaphor: comparing the muse to a summer’s day. What’s an important image? The image of winds shaking a tree in summer: “Rough winds to shake the darling buds of May”. It shows us how even summer can have storms: even something we think is perfect can have flaws. What’s an important quote? “So long as men can breathe or eyes can see So long lives this, and this gives life to thee.” This shows us (in such gorgeous language!) that the muse will live forever through this poem: a summer’s day will end, but he will live as long as the poem is read.
