Onomatopoeia:

​
A word that sounds like the sound it makes.
​
Example: Buzz, splat, Miaow, moo.

Alliteration:

Alliteration is the repetition of the same letter at the beginning of a word. ​

Example: Lake water lapping with low sounds by the shore ​

Assonance:

Assonance is the repetition of similar vowel sounds. ​

Example: Hear the mellow wedding bells. ​

Personification:

​Personification is when an inanimate object is given human-like qualities. ​

Example: The stars winked in the night sky. ​

Metaphor:

A Metaphor is a comparison between two things without using the words like, as or than. ​
​
Example: ​ ​
“Hope” is the thing with feathers -​​
That perches in the soul -​​
And sings the tune without the words -​​
And never stops - at all -​

Simile:

A simile is a comparison between two things using the words ‘like’, ‘as’ or ‘than’. ​​
​
Example: ​ ​
My mistress’ eyes are nothing like the sun;​
Coral is far more red than her lips’ red. ​
