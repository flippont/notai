Séimhiú

A séimhiú (or lenition in English), is a special gramatical rule where you place the letter "h" after the first letter of a consonant.
The séimhiú is used in :
- Prepositions
- Numbers
- adverbs/conjunctions
- Possesive adjectives

Prepositions

The séimhiú is used in some prepositions. Here is a list:
Ar, De, Do, Don, Faoi, Ó, Trí
E.g. (Ó chara - From a friend)

Numbers

The numbers 1-6 put a séimhiú on the word that comes after.
E.g. (ceithre mhadra - four dogs)

Adverbs and conjunctions

Adverbs and conjunctions like too and when put a séimhiú on the word that comes after.
E.g. (sa bhaile - in the house)

Possesive adjectives

Singular possesive adjectives (mo, do and a) put a séimhiú on the following word.
E.g. (mo pheann - my pen)

Urú

An urú is another gramatical rule that is crucial in Irish spelling and grammar. It involves putting extra letter(s) at the begining of the word. The letters that take an urú are
b > m
c > g
d > n
t > d
g > n
p > b
f > bh

The urú is used in :
- Prepositions
- Numbers
- Possesive adjectives

Prepositions

The urú is used in prepositions, when they meet wth the definite article ("the"). Here is a list:
Ar an, i, Faoi an, Ón, Trí an
E.g. (Ón gcara - From the friend)

Numbers

The numbers 7-10 put a séimhiú on the word that comes after.
E.g. (seacht bpeann - seven pens)

Possesive adjectives

Plural possesive adjectives (ár bhur and a) put a séimhiú on the following word.
E.g. (ar bpeann - our pen)
