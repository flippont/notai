The conditional tense puts a condition to any sentence. For example, the would in "I would buy a chair" would be the condition. You only add the pronouns when it is sé/sí/sibh.

One syllable verbs

In the Modh Coinníollach, you start by adding the rules of the aimsir chaite - the past tense onto the start of the verb.
Then, you add the respective endings on to the verb endings as shown below. The endings need to match the caol leathan rule.
"Fainn/Finn" = My
"Fá/Feá" = Your
"Fadh/Feadh" = His/Her
"Faimis/Fimis" = Our
"Fadh/Feadh" = Your(Plural)
"Faidís/Fidís" = Their

For the question form, you use faí/fí as the endings, and for the question, you use an + urú.

Two syllable verbs

The rules are all the same as the one syllable verbs, but different edings are added. Like the other tenses, you remove the aigh/igh and the last vowels
Here are the endings. "Eoinn/Óinn" = My
"Eofá/Ófa" = Your
"Eodh/Ódh" = His/Her
"Eoimis/Óimis" = Our
"Eodh/Ódh" = Your(Plural)
"Eoidís/Óidís" = Their
For the question form, you use eofá/ófá as the endings, and for the question, you use eofaí/ófaí.
