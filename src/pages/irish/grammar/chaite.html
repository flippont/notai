Side note

The saorbhriathar is the free verb, and it is used when someone has done something but it is unclear as to who committed the action. Since the subject is unknown, a pronoun is never added.
(E.g., Goideadh an níocháin – The washing was stolen)
Caol verbs are verbs which have a slender vowel (i.e: the letters I and E) as their last vowel
Leathan verbs are verbs which have a broad vowel (i.e: the letters A O and U) as their last vowel.
A Séimhiú is when an “H” is added after the first consonant in a verb.
The pronouns in Irish are as shown in the table below

One Syllable Verbs

To conjugate a one syllable verb, apply the following rules:
If the first letter of a verb is a vowel, a d’ before the vowel is added, except for when the verb is used to form a question, the negative form or the saorbhriathar.
If the first letter of a verb is a consonant, a séimhiú is added except for when the verb starts with either an L, N or R
The respective pronouns are added after the verb. If the pronoun is "We", the endings eann/ann are added To form a question, an Ar is added before the verb. If the verb doesn't start with a vowel, also add a séimhiú.
To form a negative statement, a Níor is added before the verb. Similar to when forming the question, a séimhiú is added when the first letter of the verb is not a vowel.
Finally, to form the saorbhriathar, eadh is added as an ending when the verb is Caol, and adh is added when the verb is Leathan

Two Syllable Verbs

The rules are nearly exactly the same as the one syllable verbs, but the sinn/muid(we) form, aíomar/íomar is used as the verb endings
Furthur more, the saorbhriathar endings change to aíodh / íodh.
