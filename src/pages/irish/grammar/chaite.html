The past tense in Irish is probably the easiest tense to remember with only 3 cases, and only one ending for the we form for one syllable verbs. Before we dive into the conjugation, let's start off with a couple basic rules.
Verbs in Irish can either be caol (slender) or leathan (broad). The broad vowels are a,o,u, and the slender vowels are i and e.

One Syllable

When conjugating a verb in the past tense in Irish, you have to a) add letters to the beginning of the verb, and b) add the respective pronouns
For the beginning, if the first letter of the verb is...
- A vowel then you add a D',
- A consonant that is not l, n, or r then you add a séimhiú(h),
- The letter "F" t then you add a séimhiú(h) and a D' (Fág => D'fhág),

There are no endings for the past tense, so you use the pronouns "Mé/tú/sé/sí/sinn(muid)/sibh/siad (I you he she we you they)", but for the sinn/muid(we) form, you use eamar/amar as the endings instead of just putting sinn/muid. (Tuig muid => Thuigeamar)
For a question, you use "Ar" and a séimhiú regardless of it's starting letter.
For the saorbhriathar (The Free Verb), use eadh/adh as the endings, without the D' or séimhiú

Two Syllables

The rules are nearly exactly the same as the one syllable verbs, but the sinn/muid(we) form, we use aíomar/íomar for the endings, and use aíodh / íodh for the saorbhriathar(The Free Verb).
