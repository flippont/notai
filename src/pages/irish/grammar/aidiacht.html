The possesive adjective is used when you own a certain noun. For example in English the possesive adjective in, "My Bag" would be "My" because it posseses the noun "Bag" onto you.
Unlike English though, the possesive adjective is also used when you want to show where you live. For example, "I live in Dublin" would be "Tá mé i mo chónaí i mBaile Átha Cliath"
The possesive adjectives are
"Mo" = My
"Do" = Your
"A" = His/Her
"Ár" = Our
"Bhur" = Your(Plural)
"A" = Their

To add a possesive adjective to a noun, you need to follow a couple of steps.
When your noun starts with the letters F, D, G, P, T, or C, add an urú
When your noun starts with a consonant then add a séimhiú to First Person adjectives (My, Your, He, She) and add an urú to Third person adjectives (Our, Your(Plural), Their)
And when your noun starts with a vowel, then the possesive adjective changes like this: "Mo" = M'
"Do" = D'
"A(His)" = Nothing
"A(Her)" = H
"Ár" = Ár Urú
"Bhur" = Bhur Urú
"A" = A Urú
For example, My apple would be M'úll, and our apple would be ár n-úll
