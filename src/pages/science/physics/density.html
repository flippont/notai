Density is the mass per unit volume, or D = M V Where D is density, M is mass and V is volume.
The standard unit of density is the k g / m 3 . The density of water is 1 g / c m 3 , since mass is centred around water.
Something will float on a fluid if it is less dense than the fluid, and vice versa. This tendency is known as buoyancy, which was discovered by Archimedes in his famous episode with the bath.
