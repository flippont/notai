Speed is the rate in which an object is moving. It is calculated with this formula S = D t when S is speed, D is distance and t is time.
The standard unit for speed is the metre per second ( m / s or m s − 1 ). Terminal velocity is the steady speed achieved by an object falling through a gas/liquid.
Velocity is the rate at which an object is moving, at a specific direction. It is calculated with V = D t when V is velocity, D is the distance in a given direction and t is time.
When a body is speeding up, it is usually said to be accelerating. It is calculated with this formula
A = δ V t when A is acceleration, δ is the change in velocity (Note: δ shows a change in something) and t is time.
On earth, air resistance exists to slow bodies down. The faster you go, the stronger the air resistance becomes. When you are in a vacuum with no air, any object regardless of their density will fall at the same rate.
