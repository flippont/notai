Work measures the energy transfer that happens when an object is moved with a force, and it is measured in Joules (J). The formula is W = F D when W is work, F is force and D is distance
Energy is the ability to do work, and it is, like work, measured in joules (J). The different forms of energy are: Kinetic, Potential (which includes elastic and gravitational), Heat, Light, Sound (which is caused by vibrations), Electrical, Chemical and magnetic.
The principal of conservation of energy states that energy cannot be created or destroyed. It can only be changed from one form, into another.
Dissipation is a term that used to describe a way in which energy is wasted, by turning into less useful forms, like heat.
Power is the amount of energy converted into another form of energy in a span of time, and it is measured in watts (w). The formula is
P = W t when P is power, W is the work done in joules and t is time.
