Algebraic Equations are equations that have letters in them, like a + 12, 10 × b, or x + y + z.

These letters are called variables, which represent another number.

For example, when you have “x” number of apples and 12 bananas, the total number of apples and bananas are x + 12.

Rules

There are a few rules to follow when thinking about algebraic equations.

Rule 1: Abbreviate the × symbol when multiplying (E.g., a × b = ab)
Rule 2: Abbreviate the 1 when multiplying by 1 (E.g., 1 × a = a)
Rule 3: Abbreviate the 1 and add a - when multiplying by -1 (E.g., -1 × a = -a)
Rule 4: when multiplying a number by a variable, the number comes first (E.g., a × 4 = 4a)

For example, if you have x number of 2-euro coins and y number of 1-euro coins, the total amount of money would be

  • The total amount of 2-euro coins, which is 2 × x or 2x (Rule 1, Rule 3), plus
  • The total amount of 1-euro coins, which is 1 × y or y (Rule 2)

Which is 2x + y.
