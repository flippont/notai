Powers

Powers are a simple way to write repeated multiplication using the same number.

When multiplying 2 four times you get 2 × 2 × 2 × 2 which is equal to 16.

In this case, the number you get after multiplying 2 four times is called "2 to the power of 4" and you write it as 24.

The 4 (The smaller digit) in this case is called the index or an exponent. The plural of index is indices. The 2 (The larger digit) is called the base.

When the index is 2, it is called squaring, and when the index is 3 it is called cubing.

Roots

√ (Roots) are numbers that have a square that is equal to the number.

For example:

42 is 16, so √16 is a 4.

52 is 25, so √25 is a 5.

62 is 36, so √36 is a 6.

These are easy to visualise when thinking them in terms of a physical square.

A square with an area of 16cm2 have side lengths of 4. This is the same as √16.
