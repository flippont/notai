Similar triangles are two triangles with the same shape but different sizes.

These triangles have a set of rules that they follow

  1. All corresponding angles in the two triangles are equal
  2. The ratio between all of the corresponding sides are the same

For example, if you have two similar triangles with side lengths a, b, c, and a1, b1 and c1, you can say that -

a/a1 = b/b1 = c/c1

There are three ways to test for similar triangles.

  • AA – If 2 corresponding angles are equal (Angle Angle)
  • SAS – If 2 pairs corresponding angle have the same ratio and the angle in between them are equal.
  • SSS – If 3 corresponding sides have the same ratio.
