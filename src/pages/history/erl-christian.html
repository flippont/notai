Early Christian Ireland was the period when Christianity first came to Ireland from a bishop named Palladius in AD 431. Before this, people in Ireland were pagan meaning that they believed in more than one God. The most famous bishop to travel to Ireland was St. Patrick who was in Ireland between AD 432 and AD 461.

Many monasteries were built from sixth century and on (E.g., Inis Mór - The First ever monastery founded in Ireland by St Enda, Clonard, Kells, Clonmacnoise, Glendalough, Sceilig Mhichil).

There are a lot of different types of monastery buildings. Here is a list of the different types.

  • Beehive Hut
    Small stone huts shaped like a beehive, where monks slept.
  • Oratories
    Where the monk attended mass or prayed
  • Scriptorium
    Where manuscripts were copied by hand and illustrated
  • Refectory
    Where monks ate their meals
  • Round tower
    A safe place for people and treasure when the monastery was under attack

A large variety of art was created by the monks. They included chalices, brooches, cups and crosses. They decorated each object with gold filigree work and enamel. A good example of an art piece from this time is Ardagh Chalice is a chalice in the National Museum of Ireland.

The Vikings who came in longships, began to raid and settle in Ireland in 8th century AD. A Longphort – a Viking camp by the water for raids – was found in wood Quay in Dublin.
