An archaeologist studies the remains left by people in the past. When an archaeologist digs the remains up left by people in the past is known as an excavation.

Here are different skills and methods used by archaeologists.

  • Geophysical Surveying
    Taking an X-ray of the ground to see what is under the ground without excavation
  • Pollen Analysis
    You can see what was growing at a certain time period by looking at pollen remains. You compare the pollen found, with a list of what type of pollen was common in a certain era, you can get a rough estimate of the period when a nearby forest was cleared / when farming began.
  • Carbon-14 dating (Radio-carbon dating)
    Due to the short half life of carbon-14, if you measure the amount of carbon-14 in an artefact you can see how old it is. Every living thing contains carbon-14, so if you take any dead animal/plant, you can see how old it is.
  • Stratigraphy
    Stratigraphy dates artefacts based on how deep underground the artefact was found in. The deeper they are, the older they will be since a small amount of soil covers up that location over time. The layers of soil is called strata, hence the name “stratigraphy”.
  • Dendrochronology
    Dendrochronology is a method of dating that uses the growth patterns of tree rings as a guide to see how old a tree is.
